### 2.1 Viral LinkedIn Post — AI/Tech Topic

**When to use:** You have a raw idea, experience, or piece of content about AI tools, workflows, or tech and want to convert it into a high-engagement LinkedIn post with emojis and hashtags.

```
Act as a social media expert specializing in tech and AI content on LinkedIn.

Here is the raw content/idea I want to post about:
[paste your raw notes, experience, or idea]

Create a viral LinkedIn post that:
- Opens with a hook that stops the scroll (bold statement, surprising fact, or relatable scenario)
- Uses emojis strategically (not excessively) to improve readability
- Has a clear structure: Hook → Context → Value → CTA
- Ends with a question to drive comments
- Includes relevant hashtags (10–15, mix of broad and niche)
- Stays within 1,200–1,500 characters for optimal reach

Tone: Professional but conversational. Authentic, not promotional.
Audience: Product managers, developers, AI enthusiasts, tech professionals on LinkedIn.
```

**Tags:** `LinkedIn` `content-creation` `social-media` `AI` `viral` `personal-brand`

### 2.2 Humanized LinkedIn Post

**When to use:** After generating a LinkedIn post, apply this follow-up prompt to make it sound more natural and human-written.

```
Apply a humanizer pass to the following LinkedIn post. Remove all signs of AI-generated writing including:
- Inflated symbolism or overly poetic language
- Promotional or salesy tone
- Vague attributions ("studies show", "experts say")
- Em dash overuse
- "Rule of three" structures that feel formulaic
- AI vocabulary words (seamless, leverage, robust, unlock, delve, game-changer, etc.)
- Excessive conjunctive phrases

Replace with:
- Conversational, direct language a real professional would use
- Specific details over vague generalizations
- Natural sentence rhythm with variation in length
- First-person voice that feels authentic

Post to humanize:
[paste LinkedIn post]
```

**Tags:** `LinkedIn` `humanizer` `content-creation` `writing` `personal-brand`

### 2.3 Hackathon / Achievement Story Post

**When to use:** You want to document a hackathon win, project milestone, or professional achievement on LinkedIn in a way that's story-driven and resonates emotionally.

```
I want to write a LinkedIn post documenting a recent achievement. Help me craft a story-driven post.

Achievement details:
- What happened: [describe the event, e.g., hackathon win, project completion]
- The challenge/obstacle we faced: [what almost went wrong or was hard]
- What we built: [product/solution name and 1-line description]
- Key people to acknowledge: [names and their contribution]
- The outcome: [result, e.g., won first place, client approved, demo went well]
- Emotion I want to convey: [e.g., resilience, team spirit, innovation]

Post requirements:
- Lead with the win but center the narrative on the journey
- Include a moment of real struggle to make it authentic
- Acknowledge teammates genuinely without sounding scripted
- End with a takeaway or lesson for the reader
- Add emojis and 10–12 relevant hashtags
- Tone: warm, human, proud but grounded
```

**Tags:** `LinkedIn` `content-creation` `storytelling` `hackathon` `achievement` `personal-brand`

### 2.4 Cinematic User Persona Story (for Product Case Studies)

**When to use:** You need an emotionally compelling persona narrative for a product case study, PM presentation, or UX research — that reads like a short story, not a bullet list.

```
You are a story creator crafting a cinematic, empathetic persona story for a product case study.

Product/feature to highlight: [e.g., Hinge's 3-match-per-day feature]
Persona name and age: [e.g., John, 25 / Jane, 35]
Region/city: [e.g., Pune, India]

Story requirements:
- Length: 250–400 words, 6–8 short paragraphs
- Arc: Before (daily struggle) → Inciting friction → Rising tension → Product as the "hero" → Transformation → After (new routine) → Hopeful close + soft CTA
- Tone: empathetic, vivid, plain language, culturally respectful to the region
- Include 1 line of natural dialogue and 1–2 italicized inner thoughts
- Name 3 concrete product mechanisms woven into the prose (e.g., "auto-sorts receipts," "predicts peak traffic")
- Avoid bullet lists, hype, or unrealistic claims
- End with a subtle, story-fitting CTA (e.g., "Tonight, she taps 'Start'.")

Output: Story only (no meta-commentary or outlines).
```

**Tags:** `content-creation` `persona` `product` `UX` `storytelling` `case-study`
